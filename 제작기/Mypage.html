1. Weekly Exercise Plan
- Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of high-intensity aerobic exercise each week.   Activities such as brisk walking, cycling, and swimming are ideal.
2. Strength Training
- Include full-body strength training exercises at least twice a week. Use weights, bodyweight exercises (push-ups, squats, etc.),

3. Flexibility Exercises
- Incorporate flexibility exercises such as yoga or stretching to maintain flexibility. This helps with balance and joint health.
4. Balance Exercises
- Practice exercises to improve balance, such as standing on one leg or balance exercises.

1. Balanced Meals
- Incorporate a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables into your diet. Aim for balanced meals with different food groups at each meal.
2. Whole Grains
- Choose whole grains (brown rice, oats, whole wheat bread) over refined grains. They are rich in fiber and nutrients.
3. Healthy Protein
- Include plant-based proteins (beans, lentils, tofu) and lean animal proteins (chicken breast, fish) in your diet.
4. Healthy Fats
- Consume foods rich in omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, walnuts, avocados) and limit intake of saturated and trans fats.
5. Adequate Hydration
- Drink plenty of water daily and avoid sugary beverages.
6. Moderate Sodium Intake
- Reduce salt intake and use herbs and spices to enhance flavor.
